# Example: Ripple Client Briefing — Adaptive Teaching

This is a real teaching conversation that shows how the skill should adapt when the context is practical (client prep) rather than academic. The depth is the same, but the focus shifts to what the learner needs for their specific situation.

## What Made This Great

- **Context changed everything** — "they are my client and I need to understand what they do before my session tomorrow" immediately shifted from academic to practical prep
- **Web research first** — searched the web to get current information before proposing curriculum
- **Curriculum adapted to context** — 4 content sections + vocabulary cheat sheet, not a deep technical dive
- **Real numbers and specifics** — $27 trillion in nostro accounts, $125M fine, $1.25B Hidden Road acquisition, SWIFT's 11,500 institutions
- **Three-layer separation** — Ripple (company) vs XRP Ledger (technology) vs XRP (token) — this was the structural insight that made everything click
- **Handled rabbit holes masterfully** — when user asked "is buying XRP a good idea?", gave the shape of the debate (bull case, bear case, structural tension) without crossing into financial advice, then redirected
- **Anticipated what the user would encounter** — "Expect a certain confidence, a sense of vindication, possibly a chip-on-the-shoulder energy" about the post-SEC-lawsuit culture
- **Named honest tensions** — didn't just sell Ripple's narrative; noted SWIFT GPI competition, modest fee revenue, Forbes "crypto zombie" critique
- **Vocabulary as safety net** — the cheat sheet ensures nothing catches her off guard in the meeting

## Curriculum Structure (Client Prep Mode)

1. The Problem Ripple Exists to Solve (SWIFT is broken — $27T parked globally)
2. What Ripple Actually Does (3 layers: company, ledger, token + bridge currency)
3. What Makes Ripple Different From Bitcoin (replace banks vs work with banks)
4. Ripple's Current Moment — 2025 (SEC resolved, acquisition spree, RLUSD, political positioning)
5. Vocabulary Cheat Sheet

## Key Adaptation Patterns

**Meeting prep vs academic learning:**
- Academic: "Let me build your mental model from the deepest constraint"
- Meeting prep: "You need enough fluency to understand context, connect with their reality, and not get blindsided by terminology"

**Handling the investment question:**
> "I'd be doing you a disservice if I just dodged the question... The honest answer is: I'm not a financial advisor. But I can give you the shape of the debate so you can form your own view."

Then: bull case, bear case, structural tension (token ≠ equity), redirect back to prep.

**The room-reading insight:**
> "Ripple exists in two worlds simultaneously — the institutional world where they sell infrastructure to banks, and the retail world where millions of individual investors are emotionally invested in XRP's price."

This helped the user understand the cultural context she'd be walking into.
